On reasons

“I can’t be happy,” he said.
“Why can’t you be happy?” asked Courage.
“I don’t know how to,” he replied, “I have no reason to be.”
“You don’t need a reason to be happy,” replied Courage.
The man was distraught.
“But how can you just be happy? How can you live your life knowing that the forces that dictate your happiness are decided on a whim, completely outside of your own control and that you will never find permanence in this world and in this life and perhaps not even in the next? How can you be happy to let the universe take you places through a fate you cannot even decide for yourself?”
“I’m sorry, I don’t understand what you just said,” smiled Courage cheerfully.
